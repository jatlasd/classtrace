# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ClassTrace is for individual teachers who need to capture student-specific evidence while they are moving through real classroom work. The strongest early users are special education teachers, case managers, interventionists, resource teachers, co-teachers, and teachers with heavy documentation needs. They use the app in short, practical moments: after noticing support, progress, behavior, participation, confusion, or follow-up needs, and before that evidence disappears into memory.

## Product Purpose

ClassTrace helps teachers turn messy, fast classroom observations into organized student evidence without removing teacher judgment. The core loop is class-first roster setup, student-specific capture, structured draft review, teacher-approved evidence, saved timeline, and readable student reporting. Success means a teacher can quickly capture what happened, validate the deterministic interpretation, and later find trustworthy evidence for one student.

ClassTrace is not a general teacher notebook, gradebook, SIS, IEP-writing system, parent communication tool, district data warehouse, admin dashboard, surveillance system, or AI documentation generator.

## Brand Personality

Calm, practical, trustworthy.

The product voice should feel like a clear teacher workspace: supportive without being cute, professional without becoming corporate, and direct enough to protect the evidence workflow. It should use plain teacher language such as Capture, Evidence feed, What happened?, Review before saving, Student, Tags, Follow-up, and Timeline.

## Anti-references

ClassTrace should not look or feel like an enterprise analytics dashboard, SIS, gradebook, compliance factory, gamified classroom app, child-facing product, social feed, generic SaaS template, or AI-powered documentation tool. Avoid fake controls for workflows that do not exist, admin/reporting-heavy navigation, bright rainbow tag systems, compliance overclaims, AI language, and designs that make teachers start from reports or student profiles instead of fast capture.

## Design Principles

1. Keep capture first.
2. Make teacher validation unmistakable.
3. Protect the one-student evidence model.
4. Prefer calm, scannable work surfaces over dashboard drama.
5. Stay honest about what is stored, inferred, and teacher-approved.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

ClassTrace should meet practical WCAG-aligned product UI expectations: readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-usable dialogs and controls, labeled inputs, accessible icon-only buttons, readable inline errors, and mobile/desktop usability. Reduced motion should be respected, color must not be the only indicator of state, and destructive actions must be visually and verbally clear. The interface should avoid compliance claims unless those have been separately reviewed.
